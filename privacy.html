Privacy Policy

       
General Information

At areamerchant.co, we are committed to protecting your privacy and safeguarding your personal data in compliance with global data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your data when you access or interact with our website and its features. We believe in complete transparency and want you to feel safe and informed while using our services.

You may browse our website without registering or submitting any personal data. However, if you engage with certain features—such as submitting forms, subscribing to content, or accessing third-party offers—we may collect specific information needed to provide those services effectively. This policy applies to all visitors, users, partners, and third parties who engage with areamerchant.co.

Contact Information

For any privacy-related inquiries, data access requests, or questions about this policy, please contact our Data Protection Officer via email at support@areamerchant.co or call us at 888 618 9285. We aim to respond to all requests within a reasonable timeframe in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

Personal Data We Collect

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us, such as your name, email address, phone number, and preferences when subscribing to newsletters, filling out forms, or interacting with embedded features. We also collect technical data such as IP addresses, browser type, device identifiers, referring pages, time stamps, and usage patterns. This data helps us optimize your user experience, prevent fraud, and improve our website’s functionality.

Categories of Data Processed

The following categories of personal and technical data may be processed through our platform:

– Identification data (e.g., name, email address, country)
– Usage data (e.g., visited pages, click activity, session duration)
– Device and browser metadata (e.g., IP address, operating system, device type)
– Affiliate interaction data (e.g., outbound clicks, referral IDs)
– Communication data (e.g., responses via contact forms or support chat)

Legal Grounds for Data Processing

We process your personal data in accordance with the following legal bases under Article 6 of the GDPR:
– Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) – when you actively agree to share your information.
– Contractual necessity (Art. 6(1)(b)) – when data is required to fulfill a service or request.
– Legal obligations (Art. 6(1)(c)) – where required by law or regulation.
– Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) – such as site security, analytics, and marketing analysis.

Purpose of Data Collection

We collect and use your data for several legitimate purposes, including:
– Providing and personalizing content and services.
– Measuring and analyzing website performance.
– Responding to your requests and inquiries.
– Sending relevant marketing or service updates (where consented).
– Ensuring the safety and security of our users and systems.
– Managing affiliate partnerships and ad tracking integrations.

Data Retention

Your data is stored only for as long as necessary to fulfill its intended purpose, or as required by applicable law. After this period, your data will be securely deleted or anonymized. You may request deletion of your data at any time, subject to lawful obligations we may have to retain it.

Affiliate Program Transparency

areamerchant.co participates in affiliate marketing programs, meaning we may earn a commission if you engage with or purchase products/services through external links featured on our website. To track these interactions, we may use cookies or unique affiliate IDs. These do not reveal personal identity but may register activity (such as clicks or purchases) to properly allocate partner commissions. This tracking helps us operate the website and continue providing free content without storing your identifiable information.

Third-Party Services and Tools

We may integrate third-party tools such as analytics services (e.g., Google Analytics), advertising platforms, embedded content (e.g., YouTube, social media), and security software. These services may set their own cookies or collect data in accordance with their respective privacy policies. We encourage users to review those external policies for more insight into how your information is handled outside our direct control.

Your Rights Under Data Protection Law

As a user, you have the right to:
– Request access to the personal data we hold about you.
– Request correction or deletion of inaccurate or outdated data.
– Withdraw your consent at any time.
– Object to data processing under certain conditions.
– Request data portability in a structured format.
– File a complaint with a data protection authority if your rights are violated.

Security and Protection

We implement industry-standard security measures—such as SSL encryption, secure server infrastructure, access restrictions, and regular audits—to protect your data from unauthorized access, misuse, or loss. While no system is entirely immune to risk, we are committed to continually monitoring and improving our protection mechanisms.

Policy Changes and Updates

We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in technology, legislation, or our internal practices. Any major revisions will be clearly posted on this page and communicated where necessary. We recommend reviewing this policy periodically to remain informed about how your data is collected, used, and protected.
